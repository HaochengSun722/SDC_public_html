ABOUT PROJECT

To understand the impact of COVID-19 on public transport in Lonodn, we select the London Underground usage data and the number of reported COVID-19 cases at the borough level, which are closely related to public travel. We use regression analysis to explore the relationship between cumulative number of reported cases and the decline in the London Underground journeys for each borough. Then we use a time-series clustering algorithm to identify different use time distributions. Through investigating the time changes of station entry/exit counts, we conclude a kind of new temporal characteristics in the flow at different metro stations under the COVID-19. The results of our analysis and the visualisations seen on the website show how the London Underground usage profiles change over time before and during the COVID-19.

This study can provide government and related industries with a reference about COVID-19’s impact on the London Underground usage demand and the new temporal characteristics of the underground journeys to improve related traffic policies and optimize the operation of the London Underground during the pandemic.

Our Team

We are five students from the Bartlett Centre for Advanced Spatial Analysis, University College London. We use the data collection, processing and storage and data visualization skills learned in the class to develop this website, which contains a series of interactive operations that convey the analysis and vision derived from the changes in London Underground usage data before and during the COVID-19.
