INTRODUCTION

"At the height of rush hour, people on the London Underground usually say 'excuse me'." Now we dont't have to, since there is no more rush hour.

This project focus on the change in the London Underground traffic before and during the COVID-19 and investigate new temporal characteristics in the flow pattern at different metro stations under the pandemic by applying time-series clustering algorithms and regression analysis. We also try to identify the importance and use differences of stations during different time periods of working days and weekends. Besides, the impact of COVID-19 on the underground in different boroughs and tube lines is also explored.
