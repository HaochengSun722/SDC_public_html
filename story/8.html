Decline of Journeys in Boroughs

There are 33 boroughs in London, 30 of which have tube stations, while 'Sutton,' 'Bexley' and 'Kingston upon Thames' have no tube stations. The boroughs with tube stations have all seen their journeys drop to between 1/7 and 2/3 of the original numbers after the pandemic, with the largest drops in 'Westminster' and 'City of London' and mildest drops in 'Bromley ' and 'Barking and Dagenham.' Similar to tube lines, some boroughs have a less obvious morning and evening peak, such as 'Westminster' and 'City of London', but some boroughs still have a more obvious morning and evening peaks, such as 'Barking and Dagenham' which because these boroughs have more residential areas and jobs.
